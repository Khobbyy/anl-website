Road Show Exhibition On Tree Trunks And Branches

In a celebration of artistry that transcends boundaries, Art and Leisure-EB proudly spotlights the recent exhibition curated by our esteemed CEO, Samuel Adentwi Bentum. The showcase, centered around the transformative beauty of tree trunks and branches, serves as a testament to our commitment to fostering creative expression.Join us in reliving the captivating moments of this exceptional exhibition, where Samuel's ingenuity has left an indelible mark on the canvas of our institution's art advocacy. Some of the art works on display are as follows.

Fontom in the Akan literally means Comfort. The Akan culture endorses the African philosophy charity. The philosophy soothes the agony of the weak and the poor through a support system in which the wealthy absorb the financial difficulties of the needy. Based on this belief, the weak and the poor, the aged and vulnerable, the destitute insolvent and others are continually maintained by the strong, privileged and generous.
Fontom is a composition of a female figure resting on her back. The wood sculpture is produced from half o f a tree trunk that has projection facing the top right part. This projection represents the head of the figure. Her elbows are stretched backwards. The upper arms of the stretches along the sides of the torso as well as across the tummy. Both knees are raised high, swung to the left and connects perfectly to the bulged foot and the buttocks.

Ena no do is a composition of a mother and child in romance.The mother represents the community and the child stand for the indigenes and the community members. The mother is set in a reclining pose and has her legs raised high in the diagonal position. The child is fused to the body of the mother leaving the head stand out. The heads of the actors are cast in cordiality exposing the love within them. This composition reveals the bound between mothers and their children.

Itu a Ennkopɛm expresses the readiness of the Akan and African youth to step forward, strive and persevere, and forge through all the odd to achieve excellence.The composition seeks to encourage the youth to resist all social, political, economic, educational and cultural barriers that impede human progress.The composition is made from a tree trunk that is attached its roots. It depicts a youthful male in a swift movement. The sculpture is posed in an s-curve pattern the creates dynamism.

As we continue our journey in art advocacy, we look forward to more collaborations that inspire, elevate, and celebrate the diverse expressions of creativity. The convergence of nature's elegance and our CEO's creative vision has not only enriched our institution's legacy but has also reaffirmed our commitment to fostering a vibrant artistic community.
