STMA and Palermo Delegation's Grand Visit Art and Leisure-EB

In a monumental stride towards fostering international collaboration, a distinguished seven-member delegation from Palermo, Italy, graced the Sekondi-Takoradi Metropolitan Assembly (STMA) in the pursuit of the EU-funded Twin-Cities in Sustainable Partnership Project (TCSPP)

The Delegation was led by Mr. John Laste (Metro Public Relations Officer and TCSPP Communication and Visibility Officer) and Mr. Ebenezer Ackom (Metro Director, Centre for National Culture). The thematic focus of the project seeks to promote culturally-sensitive and inclusive urban strategies between Sekondi-Takoradi and Palermo in the field of sustainable development through each other's cultural heritage. This seeks to valorize the vibrant arts and culture landscape in the two cities to spur socio-economic development.

In view of the above, representatives from Palermo Municipality, were treated to an enlightening exploration of Art and Leisure-EB hotel's Art Gallery. The focal point of this captivating journey was the extraordinary art collection curated by none other than Samuel Adentwi Bentum, the CEO of the facility.

The artworks stood as a testament to the rich cultural tapestry of the country. The gallery showcased a symphony of colors and forms, each piece intricately crafted to depict the vibrant essence of Ghanaian heritage. They were guided through a visual narrative that unfolded the stories embedded in each masterpiece, offering a profound insight into the cultural nuances and artistic brilliance of the art works. Art and Leisure-EB's dedication to preserving and celebrating the country's heritage was evident in every stroke and curve, creating a harmonious blend of tradition and creative.
